Preface

Hey, welcome to my page!

Once upon a time, this place was filled with pretentious text matching the neo-noir aesthetic of a dark theme, just like any typical IT newbie’s page. But all that wordy nonsense has been thrown into the trash. Right now, this page simply exists, because, at the very least, it's cool and procedurally generated.

Here, you can check out the technologies I’m familiar with, the resources I’ve mastered, and some of my projects.

Though I stepped away from web development due to its insanely competitive nature—shamefully abandoning my attempts, I haven’t given up on programming entirely. Instead, I’ve decided to explore other areas.

My plans:

  • returning to C#, the good old language where my programming journey began;
  • taking a quick peek at Python, I’m not exactly a fan, but I can’t deny its usefulness;
  • digging deeper into Unity, after all, every boy, from age zero to eighty, dreams of making his own video game. That was once my motivation to start coding in the first place.

The future is uncertain, but a samurai has no goal, only the path, what doesn’t kill us makes us stronger, and a hundred more motivational quotes I won’t list here :)

Now, if you feel like exploring, you’ll find a bunch of information below, which I’ve tried to make as engaging as possible. Over time, I’ll add more interesting stuff, but for now, it is what it is.

Make yourself at home!

A static application developed with Next.js and Redux, combining a text reader and a slideshow display. It offers basic text formatting options such as color, size, line spacing, font family, and margins, along with background and slideshow customization.

The application supports both light and dark themes and includes an autosave feature for user preferences.

Additionally, a standalone Android .apk version, built with Capacitor, is available.

Canvas Duel

This project is a 2D game featuring two symbolically represented characters who engage in a duel, moving vertically up and down along opposite sides of the screen, similar to paddles in a classic arcade game. The player can use the mouse to rebounce the characters.

Both characters are customizable: a menu opens on click, allowing the player to change the characters' colors, as well as the colors of their shots, movement speed, shot speed, and firing rate.

Nightwrap

A minimalistic Windows desktop service that conceals the screen with a black screensaver at a specified time interval if no input is detected. Designed for users whose PC locks, logs out, or shuts down immediately after the screen switches off.

The service supports specifying the interval, operates in the system tray, and can be set to load on Windows startup

Repository:

ReaderX

A Windows desktop app designed to display text over changeable background images dynamically loaded from a specified folder. It combines reader and slideshow functions.

The app also provides font formatting, options to set random or sequential order of the slideshow with adjustable intervals, selection of specific backgrounds from a list, and support for hotkeys.
